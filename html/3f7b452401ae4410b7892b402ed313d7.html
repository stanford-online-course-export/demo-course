Staff Graded Assignments are a new component type that allows students to upload a file to the platform and be graded manually by course staff. Students receive a score and, optionally, instructor comments and an annotated file.

Enabling Staff Graded Assignments

To allow Staff Graded Assignments in a course, go to the Advanced Settings page through the Settings menu in Studio. In the first setting, titled Advanced Module List, add “edx_sga” between the brackets. Include the quotation marks. Click the Save Changes button on the bottom right once it pops up.



Adding Staff Graded Assignments to a course

To add a Staff Graded Assignment to a Unit, click the “Advanced” component button and select Staff Graded Assignment. Click the Edit button to open the settings for the component.

The “display_name” field reflects what students will see when they hover their cursor over the navigation bar at the top of the page. “Maximum score” is fairly self-explanatory – this is what student responses will be graded out of. “Problem Weight” defines the weight of the problem in relation to the other problems in the same Unit. (This component was developed by open-source contributors, so it is slightly lacking in visual polish at the moment.)

 

If this seems complicated at first, consider a hypothetical. Imagine a Unit with three multiple choice questions, each worth one point, and one Staff Graded Assignment, with a maximum score of 100. By default, the total number of points for that Unit is 103. If you would rather have the Staff Graded Assignment be worth the same as the three multiple choice questions put together, you have two options. One would be to set the Maximum score to be 3. Alternatively, to preserve more flexibility with grading, you could keep the Maximum score at 100 and change the Problem Weight to be 3. Either of these methods would change that Unit to have 6 total possible points.



Once you have created your Staff Graded Assignment, publish the Unit by changing its visibility to Public. The Staff Graded Assignment component appears to be a blank box once the draft is public. To see the settings again, click the “edit a draft” link on the right side of Studio.



Important: Note that you cannot currently have more than one Staff Graded Assignment in a Unit. If you try to have more than one, uploads will not work for any of them. In addition, you will start seeing Studio errors and have to delete the entire Unit. This limitation only applies to individual Units; it is fine to have multiple Staff Graded Assignments in a course or subsection as long as they are spread out in different Units.



Scoring a Staff Graded Assignment

To grade student submissions, first go to the Unit containing the Staff Graded Assignment in the live course. You can do this easily by clicking the View Live button on the Unit page in Studio. Once in the live course, make sure you are in Staff view mode. You can toggle between the Staff and Student views by clicking on the text (either red or green) on the top right of the page in the live course. Once you are in the Unit, click the Grade Submissions button on the bottom right of the Staff Graded Assignment component to open the grading modal. Click the “Enter grade” link to submit a grade and (optionally) comments for each student. You can use the Remove Grade button here to allow a student to upload a new file after they have been graded. You can also upload an annotated file with the button on the right.
