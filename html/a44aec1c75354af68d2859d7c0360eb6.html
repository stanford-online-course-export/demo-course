10. Processes with Recycle Streams: Ammonia Production

 

Learning Objectives:

  • Understand PFD of an ammonia production process
  • Understand the logic of recycle streams in steady state processes
  • Analyze steady state processes with recycle streams
  • Understand the logic of a purge stream in a steady state process

 

Chemical Processes with Recycle Streams:

  • Typically comprised of reactor + separator.
  • The products are separated from unused reactant(s), which are returned upstream.

 

Why recycle?

  • Reduce waste of unused reactants.  One measure of the utilization of reactants that we can use is “fractional conversion”:

\[ fractional\ conversion=\ \frac{moles\ reactant\ \bf{reacted}}{moles\ reactant\ \bf{fed}} \]

  • Some reactions have unfavorable equilibrium constants. Even when run to their natural completion, the conversion of reactants to products is incomplete. One example is ammonia synthesis.

 

Ammonia:

What is Ammonia used for?

  • Ammonia is commonly used in fertilizers, household cleaners, refrigeration, food preservatives, stimulants, textiles, lifting gas, and woodworking.
