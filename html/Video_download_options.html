Videos can be added to your course, and are a great way to engage students in multiple ways.  You can:

  • add videos that are followed by discussion components
  • insert quizzes into videos by breaking up a video into parts with questions between
  • offer downloadable versions of your videos so that students with slow internet connections can watch them offline, or so that students can store these videos for future access if so inclined
  • display captions alongside your video
  • offer downloadable transcripts of the video

In order to add videos to your course, you'll need to first upload them to YouTube.  Once there, you can then upload them to your Stanford Box.com account and reach out to the Course Ops and Production teams for further assistance with getting download links and captions for your videos.

To learn more about adding video components to your course, see the EdX documentation here.
